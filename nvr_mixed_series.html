Mixed Series (Odd One Out)

What is a Mixed Series?

A Mixed Series, also known as Classification or "Odd One Out," presents a group of figures. Unlike a standard series where you find the next item, here you must identify the one figure that does not fit the pattern or rule shared by all the other figures in the group.

How to Solve Mixed Series Puzzles

The key is to find a common property among the majority of figures and then spot the one that violates this rule.

  • 1. Form a Hypothesis: Look at all the figures and find a single, consistent feature shared by most (usually four out of five).
  • 2. Test the Hypothesis: Apply your rule to every figure. The one that doesn't conform is the answer.
  • 3. Check Common Properties: If you are stuck, systematically check for patterns based on:
    • Number of Elements: sides, lines, dots, shapes.
    • Rotation & Reflection: Are some figures just rotated versions of others, while one is a mirror image?
    • Position: location of elements (inside/outside, corner/edge).
    • Shading & Size: filled/unfilled, large/small elements.
    • Symmetry: presence or absence of a line of symmetry.
    • Figure Type: curved vs. straight lines, open vs. closed figures.
