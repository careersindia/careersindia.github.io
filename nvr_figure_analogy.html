Figure Analogy

What is Figure Analogy?

Figure Analogy problems present a relationship between two figures and ask you to find a similar relationship between a third figure and one of the options. The format is typically A : B :: C : ?, which reads "A is to B as C is to what?"

How to Solve Figure Analogy Problems

  1. Analyze the First Pair (A to B): This is the most critical step. Identify every single change that occurs when transforming Figure A into Figure B. Look for common transformations like:
    • Rotation: Clockwise or counter-clockwise movement.
    • Reflection: Flipping horizontally or vertically.
    • Size/Shading: Elements get larger/smaller or become filled/unfilled.
    • Addition/Deletion: Elements are added or removed.
    • Position Change: Elements swap places or move to new locations.
    • Shape Transformation: A polygon becomes a circle, etc.
  2. Formulate a Precise Rule: State the transformation as a clear rule. For example, "The entire figure rotates 90 degrees clockwise, and the inner shape becomes filled."
  3. Apply the Rule to Figure C: Take Figure C and apply the exact same transformation rule you just formulated.
  4. Find the Match: Your resulting figure should match one of the answer options exactly.
