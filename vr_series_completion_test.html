Series Completion

1. Theory: About Series Completion

Series Completion is a topic in logical reasoning where you are given a sequence of numbers, letters, or both, which follows a certain pattern. Your task is to identify this underlying pattern and find the missing term or the next term in the sequence. This is a very common topic in Indian competitive exams (Banking, SSC, Railways, etc.) as it directly tests a candidate's pattern recognition, logical deduction, and calculation speed.

How to Approach Series Completion Questions

  1. Observe the Trend: Look at the series. Does it increase or decrease? Is the change slow and steady (suggesting addition/subtraction) or rapid (suggesting multiplication/division, squares/cubes)? Is it an alternating up-and-down pattern?
  2. Find the Difference (First-Level): This is the most common first step. Calculate the difference between consecutive terms. If the differences are constant, it's a simple arithmetic series.
  3. Find the Difference of Differences (Second-Level): If the first-level differences are not constant, calculate the difference between them. This often reveals a hidden pattern.
  4. Check Ratios: If differences don't work, find the ratio (divide a term by its previous term). If the ratio is constant or follows a pattern, it's a geometric or mixed series.
  5. Look for Squares & Cubes: Always be on the lookout for numbers that are perfect squares (1, 4, 9, 16...) or cubes (1, 8, 27, 64...), or numbers that are related to them (e.g., n² + 1, n³ - n).
  6. Check for Alternating Patterns: Sometimes, a series is a combination of two independent series. Check the pattern of alternate numbers (1st, 3rd, 5th... and 2nd, 4th, 6th...).

Types of Series

  • Number Series: The sequence consists only of numbers.
  • Alphabet Series: The sequence consists of letters, and the pattern is based on their alphabetical position.
  • Alpha-Numeric Series: A mix of numbers and letters, where both may have independent or interconnected patterns.
  • Continuous Pattern Series: A long string of letters with repeating blocks (e.g., a_b_c_a_b_c...).

    C) 29
    D) 23

    Diagram (Pattern Analysis)

    Explanation

    This is a simple arithmetic series with a constant difference of +4 between consecutive terms.
