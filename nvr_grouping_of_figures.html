Grouping of Figures (Similarities & Differences)

1. Theory: What is Grouping of Figures?

Grouping of Figures is a non-verbal reasoning task where you must classify a set of figures into distinct groups based on a shared characteristic or property. The challenge is to identify the hidden rule—be it shape, size, rotation, number of elements, or another attribute—that unites the figures within each group.

How to Solve:

  1. Scan All Figures: Get a quick overview of all the items to spot any obvious patterns.
  2. Pick a Figure and Find Matches: Start with figure (1) and search for other figures that share a clear similarity with it. This forms your first potential group.
  3. Form the Next Group: Choose a figure not in the first group and repeat the process to find its partners.
  4. Verify the Last Group: The remaining figures should automatically form the third group. Check if they also share a common property.
  5. Match with Options: Compare your identified groups with the given answer choices.

Common Properties to Look For: Number of lines/sides, shape type, open/closed figures, shading, position of elements, symmetry, intersections, and rotation.
