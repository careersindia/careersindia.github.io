BLOOD RELATIONS

Theory: Introduction

INTRODUCTION

Blood relations means the connection between two people by blood or basically by birth. In addition to this, two people or two families can be related by marriage, hence the relations which are created by marriage are also considered as blood relations.

Blood relations can be best understood with the help of a family tree. A Family Tree is a pictorial or visual representation of our lineage. It can be considered to be like a tree where we can imagine our ancestors to be the roots, their children to be the trunk, grandchildren as branches, great grandchildren as leaves and so on.

A family tree, not only gives us a better understanding of our lineage but it also helps us to understand our relationship with different people who have common ancestors.

Theory: Question Types

TYPE OF QUESTIONS

TYPE 1: QUESTIONS BASED ON LANGUAGE

In this type of questions, relationship between two persons is given through a series of relationships involving some other people. Candidates have to go through the series of relationships and establish the relation between the two concerned persons.

Question: Example 1

Introducing a man to her husband, a woman said, “His brother's father is the only son of my grandfather". How is the woman related to the man?

  1. Mother-in-law
  2. Sister
  3. Daughter
  4. Sister-in-law

Explanation: Solution to Example 1

In this question we first make a family tree based on the given relations. Here the male members are represented by a box and the female members are represented by a circle. 'M' stands for man, 'B' stands for his brother, ‘F' stands for his father and 'GF' stands for grandfather.

  • "The only son of my grandfather" refers to the woman's father.
  • "His brother's father" refers to the man's own father.
  • So, the statement becomes: "The man's father is the woman's father."
  • If they have the same father, the man and the woman are siblings.

Studying the family tree, we understand that the man's grandfather is also the woman's grandfather. Hence, the woman is the man's sister.

So, the correct option is (b) Sister.

Question 2

Pointing to a man in a photograph, Simran said," His grandmother's only daughter is my mother." How is the man related to Simran?

  1. Father
  2. Cousin
  3. Brother
  4. Nephew
Answer

(c) Brother

Explanation

"His grandmother's only daughter" is the man's mother (as she is the only daughter). Simran says this person is "my mother". Therefore, the man and Simran share the same mother, making them siblings. The man is Simran's brother.

Question 3

Looking at a photograph, a person said, “I have no brother or sister but that man's father is my father's son". At whose photograph was the person looking at?

  1. His son's
  2. His nephew's
  3. His father's
  4. His own
Answer

(a) His son's

Explanation

The speaker says "I have no brother or sister", meaning he is an only child. "My father's son" can therefore only be the speaker himself. The statement simplifies to "that man's father is me". If the man's father is the speaker, then the man in the photograph is the speaker's son.

Question 4

Pointing to an old man, Rahul said, "His daughter is my son's aunt." How is the old man related to Rahul?

  1. Brother
  2. Father
  3. Grandfather
  4. Uncle
Answer

(b) Father

Explanation

"My son's aunt" is Rahul's sister (assuming a blood relation, not by marriage). So, the statement becomes "The old man's daughter is my sister". If the old man's daughter is Rahul's sister, then the old man is their father. Therefore, the old man is Rahul's father.

Question 5

A man introduces a woman as the daughter of the brother of his mother. How is the man related to the woman?

  1. Nephew
  2. Uncle
  3. Cousin
  4. Son
Answer

(c) cousin

Explanation

"The brother of his mother" is the man's maternal uncle. "The daughter of the brother of his mother" is the daughter of his maternal uncle. The daughter of one's uncle is their cousin. Therefore, the man is the woman's cousin.

Question 3

If A is the brother of B. C is the father of D. E is the mother of B. A and D are brothers. Then, what is the relation of E to C?

  1. Sister
  2. Sister-in-law
  3. Niece
  4. Wife
Answer

Correct answer: (d) Wife

Explanation

A and B are siblings. A and D are brothers. Thus, A, B, and D are all siblings. E is the mother of B, so E is the mother of all three. C is the father of D, so C is the father of all three. Since C is the father and E is the mother of the same children, they are husband and wife.
