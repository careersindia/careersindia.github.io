Dice Problems

Key Rules for Solving Dice Problems

  • Rule 1 (One Common Face): When two positions of a die show only one common face, the other faces are opposite to each other in a clockwise direction starting from the common face. The remaining unseen number is opposite the common number.
  • Rule 2 (Two Common Faces): When two positions of a die show two common faces, the third, non-common faces are always opposite each other.
  • Rule 3 (No Common Face):
    • Standard Dice: If the sum of any two adjacent faces is NOT 7, it is a standard die. The sum of opposite faces is always 7 (1 is opposite 6, 2 is opposite 5, 3 is opposite 4).
    • Non-Standard Dice: If the sum of any two adjacent faces IS 7, it is a non-standard die. In this case, the top face is opposite the top face, the front is opposite the front, and the side is opposite the side of the other position.

Example Questions

Question 1

QUESTION: Two positions of a die are shown: (6, 4, 1) and (5, 2, 3). Find the number opposite to 4.

ANSWER: 2

EXPLANATION: There are no common faces. We check if it's a standard die. In the first position, 6 + 1 = 7. Since adjacent faces sum to 7, it is a non-standard die. We apply the rule for non-standard dice: the corresponding faces are opposite. The front face (4) is opposite the other front face (2).

Question 2

QUESTION: Two positions of a die are shown: (1, 6, 3) and (4, 5, 2). Find the number opposite to 2.

ANSWER: 6

EXPLANATION: There are no common faces. In the first position, 1 + 6 = 7, and in the second, 5 + 2 = 7. This confirms it is a non-standard die. Applying the rule, corresponding faces are opposite: Top (1) is opposite Top (4), Side (3) is opposite Side (5), and Front (6) is opposite Front (2).

Question 3

QUESTION: Two positions of a die are shown: (2, 6, 4) and (1, 5, 3). Find the number opposite to 6.

ANSWER: 1

EXPLANATION: There are no common faces. We check the sums of adjacent faces: 2+6=8, 6+4=10, 2+4=6. None sum to 7, so this is a standard die. In a standard die, opposite faces must sum to 7. Therefore, the number opposite to 6 is 1 (since 6 + 1 = 7).

Question 4

QUESTION: Two positions of a die are shown: (3, 5, 1) and (3, 4, 2). Find the number opposite to 3.

ANSWER: 6

EXPLANATION: The number 3 is the only common face. We use the clockwise rule. Starting from 3 in the first die, we go clockwise: 3 → 5 → 1. In the second die: 3 → 4 → 2. This means 5 is opposite 4, and 1 is opposite 2. The only remaining number, 6, must be opposite the common face, 3.

Question 5

QUESTION: Two positions of a die are shown: (1, 2, 3) and (1, 5, 4). Find the number opposite to 1.

ANSWER: 6

EXPLANATION: The number 1 is the only common face. Applying the clockwise rule: from the first die, we have 1 → 2 → 3. From the second die, we have 1 → 5 → 4. Therefore, 2 is opposite 5, and 3 is opposite 4. The remaining number, 6, is opposite the common face, 1.

Question 6

QUESTION: Two positions of a die are shown: (6, 3, 2) and (6, 5, 4). Find the number opposite to 6.

ANSWER: 1

EXPLANATION: The number 6 is the only common face. Following the clockwise rule: 6 → 3 → 2 and 6 → 5 → 4. This tells us that 3 is opposite 5, and 2 is opposite 4. The unseen number, 1, must be opposite the common face, 6.

Question 7

QUESTION: Two positions of a die are shown: (6, 1, 4) and (1, 5, 6). Find the number opposite to 4.

ANSWER: 5

EXPLANATION: The numbers 1 and 6 are common to both positions. According to Rule 2, when two faces are common, the remaining non-common faces are opposite each other. Here, the remaining faces are 4 and 5, so 4 is opposite 5.

Question 8

QUESTION: Two positions of a die are shown with letters: (A, B, C) and (C, D, B). Find the face opposite to C.

ANSWER: Cannot be determined.

EXPLANATION: The letters B and C are common to both positions. Using Rule 2, we can determine that the non-common faces, A and D, are opposite each other. However, this method does not provide information about the faces opposite to the common letters (B and C). The opposite of C could be either of the two unseen letters (e.g., E or F).
