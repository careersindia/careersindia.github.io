Simplification

1. Theory: About Simplification

Simplification is the process of reducing a complex mathematical expression into its simplest form. In competitive exams in India (especially Banking - IBPS, SBI; and SSC), these questions form a major part of the Quantitative Aptitude section. They are designed to test a candidate's speed and accuracy in performing basic arithmetic operations.

While often grouped with quantitative topics, the logical application of rules makes it relevant to reasoning as well. The cornerstone of simplification is the BODMAS rule.

Core Technique: The BODMAS / PEMDAS Rule

This rule defines the correct order of operations to solve a mathematical expression.

Diagram (BODMAS Hierarchy)

B - Brackets (solve innermost first: (), {}, []) O - Of / Orders (powers, square roots, etc.) D - Division (÷ or /) M - Multiplication (* or ×) A - Addition (+) S - Subtraction (-) Note: Division and Multiplication have equal priority (solve left to right). Addition and Subtraction have equal priority (solve left to right).

Other Key Techniques

  • Approximation & Estimation: For questions asking for an approximate value, round off the numbers to the nearest convenient integers to simplify calculations.
  • Fractions & Percentages: Memorize the fractional equivalents of common percentages (e.g., 25% = 1/4, 33.33% = 1/3) to speed up calculations.
  • Digital Sum: A shortcut to verify options. The digital sum of the answer must match the digital sum of the question. (Digital sum is the sum of digits of a number until a single digit is obtained).
  • Squares, Cubes, and Roots: Memorize squares up to 30 and cubes up to 15. This saves a massive amount of time.

Types of Questions

  • BODMAS based questions: Direct application of the rule with multiple operations.
  • Approximation: Finding the approximate value of an expression involving decimals and large numbers.
  • Missing Number questions: Expressions of the form 15 * ? + 20 = 110.
  • Questions with fractions, percentages, and decimals.
  • Questions involving exponents (powers) and surds (roots).

2. Questions, Answers, and Explanations

Difficulty Level 1: Easy (Questions 1-10)

These questions involve the direct application of the BODMAS rule with integers.

  1. Question 1: 15 + 20 ÷ 5 – 2 * 3 = ?

    A) 9
    B) 13
    C) 7
    D) 23

    Answer: B) 13

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Division: 20 ÷ 5 = 4 Expression: 15 + 4 – 2 * 3 2. Multiplication: 2 * 3 = 6 Expression: 15 + 4 – 6 3. Addition: 15 + 4 = 19 Expression: 19 – 6 4. Subtraction: 19 – 6 = 13

    Explanation

    Applying BODMAS: First, we perform division (20 ÷ 5 = 4), then multiplication (2 * 3 = 6). The expression becomes 15 + 4 - 6. Then we perform addition (15 + 4 = 19) and finally subtraction (19 - 6 = 13).

  2. Question 2: (48 – 12) * 4 ÷ 2 + 6 = ?

    A) 78
    B) 18
    C) 24
    D) 60

    Answer: A) 78

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Bracket: (48 – 12) = 36 Expression: 36 * 4 ÷ 2 + 6 2. Division: 4 ÷ 2 = 2 Expression: 36 * 2 + 6 3. Multiplication: 36 * 2 = 72 Expression: 72 + 6 4. Addition: 72 + 6 = 78

    Explanation

    Applying BODMAS: First, solve the bracket (48 – 12 = 36). Expression becomes 36 * 4 ÷ 2 + 6. Division and multiplication have equal priority, so we solve from left to right. 36 * 4 = 144, then 144 ÷ 2 = 72. Finally, 72 + 6 = 78.

  3. Question 3: 100 – 5 * 10 + (25 ÷ 5) = ?

    A) 55
    B) 50
    C) 45
    D) 185

    Answer: A) 55

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Bracket: (25 ÷ 5) = 5 Expression: 100 – 5 * 10 + 5 2. Multiplication: 5 * 10 = 50 Expression: 100 – 50 + 5 3. Subtraction: 100 – 50 = 50 Expression: 50 + 5 4. Addition: 50 + 5 = 55

    Explanation

    First, solve the bracket (25 ÷ 5 = 5). Expression: 100 – 5 * 10 + 5. Next, multiplication (5 * 10 = 50). Expression: 100 – 50 + 5. Then, from left to right: 100 - 50 = 50, and 50 + 5 = 55.

  4. Question 4: 180 ÷ 9 of 2 = ?

    A) 40
    B) 10
    C) 90
    D) 1

    Answer: B) 10

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Of: 9 of 2 = 9 * 2 = 18 Expression: 180 ÷ 18 2. Division: 180 ÷ 18 = 10

    Explanation

    The operation 'Of' has higher priority than Division. So, first calculate 9 of 2 which means 9 * 2 = 18. The expression becomes 180 ÷ 18, which equals 10.

  5. Question 5: 12 * 3 + 15 – 10 = ?

    A) 36
    B) 41
    C) 21
    D) 46

    Answer: B) 41

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Multiplication: 12 * 3 = 36 Expression: 36 + 15 – 10 2. Addition: 36 + 15 = 51 Expression: 51 – 10 3. Subtraction: 51 – 10 = 41

    Explanation

    First, multiplication: 12 * 3 = 36. Then addition and subtraction from left to right: 36 + 15 = 51, and 51 - 10 = 41.

  6. Question 6: 7 + 7 ÷ 7 + 7 * 7 – 7 = ?

    A) 7
    B) 50
    C) 49
    D) 0

    Answer: B) 50

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Division: 7 ÷ 7 = 1 Expression: 7 + 1 + 7 * 7 – 7 2. Multiplication: 7 * 7 = 49 Expression: 7 + 1 + 49 – 7 3. Addition: 7 + 1 = 8 Expression: 8 + 49 – 7 4. Addition: 8 + 49 = 57 Expression: 57 - 7 5. Subtraction: 57 - 7 = 50

    Explanation

    First, division (7 ÷ 7 = 1) and multiplication (7 * 7 = 49). Expression becomes 7 + 1 + 49 - 7. Then solve from left to right: 8 + 49 = 57, 57 - 7 = 50.

  7. Question 7: 5 * 5 – 5 + 5 ÷ 5 = ?

    A) 1
    B) 5
    C) 21
    D) 25

    Answer: C) 21

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Division: 5 ÷ 5 = 1 Expression: 5 * 5 – 5 + 1 2. Multiplication: 5 * 5 = 25 Expression: 25 – 5 + 1 3. Subtraction: 25 – 5 = 20 Expression: 20 + 1 4. Addition: 20 + 1 = 21

    Explanation

    First, division (5 ÷ 5 = 1) and multiplication (5 * 5 = 25). Expression becomes 25 - 5 + 1. Then solve from left to right: 20 + 1 = 21.

  8. Question 8: 16 ÷ 4 * (2+2) = ?

    A) 2
    B) 8
    C) 1
    D) 16

    Answer: D) 16

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Bracket: (2+2) = 4 Expression: 16 ÷ 4 * 4 2. Division: 16 ÷ 4 = 4 Expression: 4 * 4 3. Multiplication: 4 * 4 = 16

    Explanation

    First, bracket: (2+2) = 4. Expression is 16 ÷ 4 * 4. Since ÷ and * have equal priority, solve left to right: 16 ÷ 4 = 4, then 4 * 4 = 16.

  9. Question 9: 360 ÷ 6 + 10 * 3 – 40 = ?

    A) 50
    B) 180
    C) 210
    D) 30

    Answer: A) 50

    Explanation

    360 ÷ 6 = 60. 10 * 3 = 30. Expression becomes 60 + 30 - 40. Solving from left to right: 90 - 40 = 50.

  10. Question 10: 50 + [10 – {4 + (2 – 1)}] = ?

    A) 53
    B) 55
    C) 57
    D) 47

    Answer: B) 55

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Innermost Bracket (): (2 – 1) = 1 Expression: 50 + [10 – {4 + 1}] 2. Middle Bracket {}: {4 + 1} = 5 Expression: 50 + [10 – 5] 3. Outer Bracket []: [10 – 5] = 5 Expression: 50 + 5 4. Addition: 50 + 5 = 55

    Explanation

    Solve brackets from the inside out. Innermost is (2-1) = 1. Then {4+1} = 5. Then [10-5] = 5. Finally, 50 + 5 = 55.

Difficulty Level 2: Moderate (Questions 11-25)

These questions involve fractions, percentages, and squares/roots.

  1. Question 11: 50% of 250 + √625 = ?

    A) 125
    B) 150
    C) 175
    D) 200

    Answer: B) 150

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Of/Order: 50% of 250 = (1/2) * 250 = 125 2. Order: √625 = 25 3. Addition: 125 + 25 = 150

    Explanation

    50% is 1/2. (1/2) * 250 = 125. The square root of 625 is 25. 125 + 25 = 150.

  2. Question 12: 1/4 + 3/8 * 16 = ?

    A) 6.25
    B) 6.5
    C) 7
    D) 7.25

    Answer: A) 6.25

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Multiplication: 3/8 * 16 = 3 * (16/8) = 3 * 2 = 6 Expression: 1/4 + 6 2. Addition: 0.25 + 6 = 6.25

    Explanation

    First, multiplication: (3/8) * 16 = 6. Then addition: 1/4 + 6 = 0.25 + 6 = 6.25.

  3. Question 13: 15² - 10² + 5³ = ?

    A) 250
    B) 200
    C) 225
    D) 250

    Answer: D) 250

    Diagram (BODMAS Steps)

    1. Orders (Powers): 15² = 225 10² = 100 5³ = 125 Expression: 225 – 100 + 125 2. Subtraction: 225 – 100 = 125 Expression: 125 + 125 3. Addition: 125 + 125 = 250

    Explanation

    First, calculate all the powers: 15²=225, 10²=100, 5³=125. The expression becomes 225 - 100 + 125. Solving from left to right: 125 + 125 = 250.

  4. Question 14: ? % of 600 = 360

    A) 40
    B) 50
    C) 60
    D) 70

    Answer: C) 60

    Diagram (Equation Setup)

    Let ? = x (x/100) * 600 = 360 x * 6 = 360 x = 360 / 6 x = 60

    Explanation

    Let the missing number be x. The equation is (x/100) * 600 = 360. This simplifies to x * 6 = 360. Solving for x, x = 360 / 6 = 60.

  5. Question 15: 1.5 * 0.2 + 5.5 = ?

    D) 5.9

  20. Question 45: Find the value of 1/(2*3) + 1/(3*4) + 1/(4*5) + ... + 1/(9*10)

    Answer: 2/5

    Explanation

    This is a telescoping series. Each term 1/(n*(n+1)) can be written as 1/n - 1/(n+1).
    The series becomes (1/2 - 1/3) + (1/3 - 1/4) + ... + (1/9 - 1/10).
    All intermediate terms cancel out, leaving 1/2 - 1/10 = 5/10 - 1/10 = 4/10 = 2/5.

  25. Question 50: What is the value of (1-1/3)(1-1/4)(1-1/5)...(1-1/100)?

    Answer: 1/50

    Explanation

    Write each term as a fraction: (2/3) * (3/4) * (4/5) * ... * (99/100).
    This is a telescoping product. The numerator of each term cancels with the denominator of the next term.
    The 3s cancel, the 4s cancel, and so on, up to the 99s.
    We are left with the first numerator (2) and the last denominator (100).
    The result is 2/100 = 1/50.
