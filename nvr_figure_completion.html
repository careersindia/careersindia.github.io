Figure Completion

What is Figure Completion?

Figure Completion problems present a large figure or matrix with a missing portion, usually a quarter of the design. Your task is to analyze the overall pattern within the main figure and select the answer choice that correctly fits into the empty space to complete the pattern.

How to Solve Figure Completion Puzzles

  1. Analyze the Overall Pattern: Look at the main figure. Is there a clear, repeating pattern? The most common patterns are based on symmetry, rotation, or simple continuation of lines.
  2. Mentally Complete the Figure: Trace the lines and shapes from the adjacent quadrants into the empty space. Imagine what the missing piece must look like to make the overall figure logical and complete.
  3. Check for Specific Rules:
    • Symmetry: The most common rule. The figure might be symmetrical horizontally, vertically, diagonally, or all of the above. The missing piece will often be a mirror image of an adjacent or opposite quadrant.
    • Rotation: A more advanced rule. The design in each quadrant might be a 90-degree rotation of the previous one.
    • Continuity: Lines, curves, and shading must continue smoothly and logically from the existing parts into the missing part.
    • Alternating Patterns: Elements might alternate in some way (e.g., filled/unfilled, circle/square) as you move around the quadrants.
  4. Eliminate and Select: Compare your mental sketch with the answer options. Eliminate choices that clearly violate the pattern, then select the one that fits perfectly.
