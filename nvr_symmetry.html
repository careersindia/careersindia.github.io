Identifying Symmetry

1. Theory: Line and Rotational Symmetry

Symmetry is a fundamental concept in geometry and non-verbal reasoning. It refers to a sense of balanced and harmonious proportion. An object is symmetric if it looks the same after a certain transformation, such as reflection or rotation.

Line of Symmetry (Reflectional Symmetry)

A figure has a line of symmetry if it can be divided by a line into two parts that are mirror images of each other. Think of it as a line along which you could fold the figure, and both halves would match up perfectly.

  • Vertical Line of Symmetry: The line runs top-to-bottom (e.g., the letter 'A').
  • Horizontal Line of Symmetry: The line runs left-to-right (e.g., the letter 'E').
  • Diagonal Line of Symmetry: The line runs corner-to-corner (e.g., a square).

A figure can have one, multiple, or no lines of symmetry.

Rotational Symmetry

A figure has rotational symmetry if it looks the same after being rotated less than a full 360° turn around its center point. The number of times the figure matches its original position during a full 360° rotation is called the order of rotational symmetry. An order of 1 means it has no rotational symmetry (as it only matches after a full 360° turn).

  • A square has rotational symmetry of order 4 (it looks the same at 90°, 180°, 270°, and 360°).
  • A rectangle has rotational symmetry of order 2 (it looks the same at 180° and 360°).
  • The letter 'S' has rotational symmetry of order 2.

How to Solve

  1. Read the Question: Determine if you need to find line symmetry, rotational symmetry, both, or a lack of symmetry.
  2. Test for Line Symmetry: For each option, mentally draw vertical, horizontal, and diagonal lines through the center. Check if the halves on either side of the line are perfect mirror images.
  3. Test for Rotational Symmetry: For each option, mentally rotate the figure around its center point. Count how many times it looks identical to the start before completing a full 360° turn.
  4. Eliminate Incorrect Options: Systematically discard the figures that do not meet the criteria specified in the question. The remaining figure is your answer.
