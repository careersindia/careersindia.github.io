Blood Relations

Core Concept: Building a Family Tree Diagram

Question

What is the most effective way to solve blood relation problems without getting confused?

Answer

By creating a visual family tree using a consistent set of symbols for gender and relationships.

Explanation

The speaker introduces a clear system of symbols to represent relationships visually:

  • Male: Represented by a Square (☐).
  • Female: Represented by a Circle (◯).
  • Married Couple (Husband & Wife): A Double Horizontal Line (═) connecting a square and a circle.
  • Siblings (Brother & Sister): A Single Horizontal Line (—) connecting two shapes.
  • Parent-Child (Generation Gap): A Vertical Line (|) connecting generations. Parents are on top, children are below.

Important Notes: The speaker emphasizes, "This is the most important part. If you learn how to make the diagram correctly, you will never get a blood relation question wrong. The diagram makes everything clear. Don't try to solve these in your head; you will get confused. Draw the diagram every time."

Type 1: Indicating Form (Pointing Questions)

Question

Pointing to a photograph, a woman says, "He is the son of the only son of my grandfather." How is the man in the photograph related to the woman?

Answer

Brother.

Explanation

To solve this, we work backward from the end of the statement:

  1. "My grandfather": This is the woman's grandfather.
  2. "The only son of my grandfather": This must be the woman's father.
  3. "He is the son of [the woman's father]": The man in the photograph is the son of the woman's father.

If the man is the son of her father, he is her brother.

Important Notes: The speaker advises, "For these 'pointing' questions, the trick is to always start from the word 'my' or 'your' and work backward. Put yourself in the place of the speaker. Who is 'my grandfather'? Then who is his 'only son'? This step-by-step process makes it simple."

Type 2: Coded Blood Relations

Question

Given the codes: `A + B` means A is the father of B; `A × B` means A is the sister of B; `A $ B` means A is the wife of B; `A % B` means A is the mother of B.
Which expression shows that M is the maternal grandmother of T?

Answer

The expression: M % N $ S + T

Explanation

We need to find an expression where M is female and two generations above T. We test the options by drawing the family tree:

  • M % N: M is the mother of N (M is female, N is below M).
  • N $ S: N is the wife of S (N is female, S is her husband).
  • S + T: S is the father of T (T is the child of S and N).

This diagram shows that M is the mother of N, and N is the mother of T. Therefore, M is the maternal grandmother of T.

Important Notes: The speaker points out a common trap: "In `A + B`, we know A is the father, but we do NOT know the gender of B. B could be a son or a daughter. Never assume the gender of the second person in the code unless it's defined by another relationship (like being a wife or husband). This is the number one mistake students make."

Type 3: Puzzle Form

Question

In a family, B is the son of C but C is not his mother. A and C are a married couple. F is the brother of C. D is the son of A. E is the brother of D. How is E related to B?

Answer

Brother.

Explanation

We build the family tree step-by-step using the symbols:

  1. "B is the son of C but C is not his mother": This means C must be the father (a square). B is his son (a square below C).
  2. "A and C are a married couple": C (father) is married to A. Therefore, A must be the mother (a circle).
  3. "F is the brother of C": F is a sibling of C (both are squares connected by a single line).
  4. "D is the son of A": D is another son of A and C.
  5. "E is the brother of D": E is a sibling of D.

The diagram shows that B, D, and E are all sons of the same parents (A and C). Therefore, E is the brother of B.

Important Notes: The speaker highlights the importance of deduction. "The clue 'C is not his mother' is the key. If C is a parent but not the mother, C *must* be the father. This is a logic puzzle hidden in a blood relation question. Always look for these deductive clues."
