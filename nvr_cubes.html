Cube Problems (Painting & Cutting)

How to Solve Cube Problems

These problems involve a large cube that is painted on its faces and then cut into smaller, identical cubes. The core of solving these problems is finding the value of 'n'.

'n' is the number of small cubes along one edge of the large cube.

n = (Side of large cube) / (Side of small cube)

If a cube is cut into 64 smaller cubes, then n³ = 64, which means n = 4.

Total number of small cubes = n³

Formulas for a Cube Painted on ALL Sides

  • Cubes with 3 Faces Painted (Corner Cubes): These are always the corner pieces. A cube has 8 corners.
    Number of 3-face cubes = 8
  • Cubes with 2 Faces Painted (Edge Cubes): These are the cubes along the edges, excluding the corners. A cube has 12 edges.
    Number of 2-face cubes = 12 * (n - 2)
  • Cubes with 1 Face Painted (Face Cubes): These are the cubes in the center of each face. A cube has 6 faces.
    Number of 1-face cubes = 6 * (n - 2)²
  • Cubes with 0 Faces Painted (Inner/Core Cubes): These form a smaller cube inside the outer layer.
    Number of 0-face cubes = (n - 2)³

Number of Cuts

To get 'n' pieces along an edge, you need 'n-1' cuts. Since cuts are made along three axes, the total number of cuts is:

Total Cuts = 3 * (n - 1)

Practice Questions

Question 1

A cube is painted red on all faces and then cut into 64 smaller, identical cubes. How many small cubes have 3 faces painted?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 8

Technique: Cubes with 3 faces painted are always the corner cubes. A cube always has 8 corners, regardless of its size or the number of cuts. The formula is constant.

3-Face Cubes = 8

Question 2

A cube is painted red on all faces and then cut into 64 smaller, identical cubes. How many small cubes have 2 faces painted?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 24

Technique: First, find 'n'. Total cubes = n³ = 64, so n = 4.
Then, apply the formula for 2-face (edge) cubes.

2-Face Cubes = 12 * (n - 2) = 12 * (4 - 2) = 12 * 2 = 24

Question 3

A cube is painted red on all faces and then cut into 64 smaller, identical cubes. How many small cubes have 1 face painted?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 24

Technique: First, find 'n'. Total cubes = n³ = 64, so n = 4.
Then, apply the formula for 1-face (face) cubes.

1-Face Cubes = 6 * (n - 2)² = 6 * (4 - 2)² = 6 * 2² = 6 * 4 = 24

Question 4

A cube is painted red on all faces and then cut into 64 smaller, identical cubes. How many small cubes have no faces painted?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 8

Technique: First, find 'n'. Total cubes = n³ = 64, so n = 4.
Then, apply the formula for 0-face (inner) cubes.

0-Face Cubes = (n - 2)³ = (4 - 2)³ = 2³ = 8

Question 5

A cube of side length 5 cm is painted blue. It is then cut into cubes of side 1 cm. How many small cubes have at least 2 faces painted?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 44

Technique: First, find 'n'. n = 5 / 1 = 5.
"At least 2 faces painted" means we need the sum of cubes with 2 faces painted and 3 faces painted.

  • 3-Face Cubes = 8
  • 2-Face Cubes = 12 * (n - 2) = 12 * (5 - 2) = 12 * 3 = 36

Total = 36 + 8 = 44

Question 10

A cube has its two opposite faces painted red, two opposite faces painted blue, and the remaining two faces painted green. It is cut into 125 smaller cubes. How many cubes have ONLY 1 face painted, and it is red?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 18

Technique: First, n³ = 125, so n = 5.
Cubes with only 1 face painted are the center pieces on each face. The formula for these is (n - 2)² per face.

  • Number of center pieces per face = (5 - 2)² = 3² = 9.
  • There are two faces painted red.

Total cubes with only red paint = 9 (from one red face) + 9 (from the opposite red face) = 18

Question 15

A cube is painted red on the top face and green on the bottom face, and left unpainted on the other four. It is cut into 27 cubes. How many cubes have no paint?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 9

Technique: n³=27, so n=3. The cube is a 3x3x3 block.
Only the top and bottom layers have paint. The entire middle layer is unpainted.
The middle layer is a 3x3 slab of cubes.
Total unpainted = 3 * 3 = 9.

Question 18

A large cube is formed by arranging 27 small cubes. If the corner cubes of the large cube are removed, how many faces of the remaining structure are exposed?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 54

Technique: Visualization and logic.

  • A 3x3x3 cube has 6 faces, each with a surface area of 9 small squares. Total surface area = 6 * 9 = 54.
  • When you remove a corner cube, you remove 3 of its exposed faces from the total surface area (-3).
  • However, cutting out that corner exposes 3 new faces on the "pit" left behind (+3).
  • The net change for removing one corner is -3 + 3 = 0.
  • Since this is true for all 8 corners, the total surface area of the remaining structure is unchanged. It is still 54.

Question 19

A cuboid of dimensions 6x4x3 cm is painted on all sides and cut into 1x1x1 cm cubes. How many cubes have exactly 2 faces painted?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 28

Technique: Cuboid Logic. The formula for 2-face cubes is (n-2) per edge. We apply this to each set of edges. A cuboid has 12 edges in 3 groups of 4.

  • 4 edges of length 6: 4 * (6-2) = 4 * 4 = 16.
  • 4 edges of length 4: 4 * (4-2) = 4 * 2 = 8.
  • 4 edges of length 3: 4 * (3-2) = 4 * 1 = 4.
Total = 16 + 8 + 4 = 28.

Question 20

Using the cuboid from Q19 (6x4x3), how many cubes have no faces painted?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 8

Technique: Cuboid Inner Core. The formula adapts to the dimensions (l, w, h). 0-Face Cubes = (l - 2) * (w - 2) * (h - 2) Calculation: (6 - 2) * (4 - 2) * (3 - 2) = 4 * 2 * 1 = 8
