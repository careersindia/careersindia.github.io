Water Images

What is a Water Image?

A Water Image is the reflection of an object into the water immediately below it. In reasoning problems, this means a horizontal reflection. The key principle is that the figure flips vertically.

How to Solve Water Image Problems

  • Left and Right Remain Unchanged: Unlike a mirror image, the left side of the object remains on the left, and the right side remains on the right. This is a critical rule for eliminating options.
  • Top Becomes Bottom (and Vice-Versa): The figure flips vertically across a horizontal line. The top part of the figure will appear at the bottom in the reflection, and the bottom part will appear at the top.
  • Proximity to the Water Line: The parts of the figure closest to the horizontal "water line" will be closest to the line in the reflection.
  • Check Orientations: An arrow pointing up (^) will point down (v) in the reflection. An arrow pointing left (<) will still point left (<).
  • Alphanumeric Characters: This is where it gets tricky. Many letters and numbers change their appearance. For example, 'A' becomes '∀', 'F' becomes 'Ⅎ'. Some, like 'C', 'D', 'E', 'H', 'I', 'O', 'X', remain unchanged if written symmetrically.
