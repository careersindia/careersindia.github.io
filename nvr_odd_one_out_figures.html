Odd One Out - Figures

What is an "Odd One Out" Puzzle?

This type of puzzle, also known as Classification or Mixed Series, presents a group of figures where all but one are alike in some way. Your goal is to identify the common rule that connects the majority of the figures and then find the single figure that violates this rule.

How to Solve These Puzzles

  1. Scan All Figures: Quickly observe all the figures in the question to get a general sense of the elements involved.
  2. Identify a Common Property: Look for a single, consistent feature that is shared by most (e.g., four out of five) of the figures. This is your potential "rule."
  3. Test Your Rule: Apply the rule to every figure. The one that does not fit is the "odd one out." If your rule doesn't exclude exactly one figure, you need to find a different rule.
  4. Common Rules to Check For:
    • Number of Elements: Count the sides, lines, dots, or shapes.
    • Rotation vs. Reflection: Check if some figures are just rotated versions of others, while one is a mirror image.
    • Position & Orientation: Note the location and direction of elements.
    • Shading & Size: Look for patterns in filled/unfilled areas or large/small elements.
    • Symmetry: Identify the presence or absence of a line of symmetry.
    • Figure Type: Notice curved vs. straight lines, or open vs. closed figures.
