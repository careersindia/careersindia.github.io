Study Abroad Process

The countries chosen commensurate with the majority of Indians travelling for the purpose of studying to a foreign country and the list is given alphabetically.

Australia
A highly regulated process centered around QEAC-certified agents, the Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement, and the subclass 500 visa.
Bahrain
Features a university-sponsored visa system where document attestation is a key prerequisite before the institution applies for the entry permit on your behalf.
Canada
Requires a Study Permit, with mandatory use of a CICC-licensed consultant for visa advice and a strong emphasis on proof of funds, often via a GIC.
China
A state-managed process requiring the original Admission Notice and JW201/JW202 form from the university before applying for the X1 entry visa.
France
Mandates a pre-consular procedure through the official Campus France "Ã‰tudes en France" platform before the VLS-TS visa application can begin.
Germany
A structured process known for its mandatory Blocked Account (`Sperrkonto`), potential APS certificate requirement, and a formal visa interview.
Italy
Involves a mandatory pre-enrollment on the Universitaly portal and academic verification via a `Dichiarazione di Valore` (DOV) or CIMEA statement.
Japan
A highly structured system where the university sponsors and applies for a Certificate of Eligibility (CoE) on the student's behalf before the visa process.
Kuwait
Operates on a university-sponsorship model where the institution applies for the entry permit after a lengthy and mandatory document attestation process.
Luxembourg
A unique two-step process requiring a "Temporary Authorisation to Stay" from within Luxembourg before the final Type D visa application can be made.
Malaysia
Centralized through the government's EMGS portal, which processes the Visa Approval Letter (VAL) before the student applies for an entry visa.
New Zealand
Verified through ENZ Recognised Agencies, the process often involves an "Approval in Principle" (AIP) from immigration before tuition fees are paid.
Oman
A sponsorship-based system where the university applies for the visa with the Royal Oman Police after the student completes a multi-stage document attestation.
Qatar
Features a university-sponsorship model where the institution handles the visa application with the Ministry of Interior after rigorous document attestation.
Russia
Requires the university to secure an official "Invitation Letter" from the Ministry of Internal Affairs before the student can apply for an entry visa.
Saudi Arabia
A university-sponsored system where the institution obtains a Visa Authorization Number from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, a prerequisite for the visa application.
Singapore
A highly regulated system using the government's SOLAR portal for the Student's Pass application, which follows an In-Principle Approval (IPA) letter.
South Korea
Requires legalization of documents (Apostille) and a mandatory embassy interview before the D-2 student visa is issued, followed by ARC registration upon arrival.
Spain
A bureaucratic process demanding document legalization via Hague Apostille, official sworn translations, and a mandatory post-arrival TIE card application.
Switzerland
A decentralized and lengthy process requiring dual approval for the visa from both the Swiss embassy and the specific cantonal migration office.
UAE
A university-sponsored system where the institution manages the visa, starting with an Entry Permit and culminating in a Residence Visa and Emirates ID.
UK
Relies on British Council certified agents, the university-issued CAS number, and a potential credibility interview for the Student Route visa.
USA
A comprehensive process involving AIRC-certified agents, standardized tests (GRE/GMAT/SAT), the university-issued I-20 form, and a critical F-1 visa interview.
