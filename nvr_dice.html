Dice Problems

How to Solve Dice Problems

Dice problems test your spatial reasoning. Unless stated otherwise, assume you are working with a standard die, where opposite faces always sum to 7 (1 is opposite 6, 2 is opposite 5, 3 is opposite 4).

Part 1: Closed Dice (Finding Opposite Faces)

When given multiple views of the same die, use these rules to find opposite faces.

Rule 1: One Face Common
If two views of a die have only one common number/symbol, the faces adjacent to the common face in each view are opposite each other. (More simply: list the numbers clockwise from the common face; the corresponding numbers in each sequence are opposites).
Rule 2: Two Faces Common
If two views of a die have two common faces, the remaining third faces in each view are opposite each other.

Part 2: Open Dice (Nets)

An open die or net is a 2D pattern that can be folded to form a cube. The key is to identify opposite faces.

The "Alternate" Rule:
In a straight strip of three or more faces, faces that are separated by one face are opposite each other (e.g., in a 1-2-3 strip, 1 is opposite 3). Apply this rule first. The last two remaining faces are opposite each other.

Section 2: Closed Dice (10 Questions)

Question 11

Two views of a die are shown. What number is on the face opposite to 3?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 6

Technique: Rule 1 (One Face Common).

  • The common face in both views is 3.
  • From die 1, the numbers adjacent to 3 are 1 and 2.
  • From die 2, the numbers adjacent to 3 are 4 and 5.
  • This means 1, 2, 4, and 5 are all adjacent to 3.
  • The only number not adjacent to 3 is 6. Therefore, 3 is opposite 6.

Question 12

Two views of a die are shown. What number is on the face opposite to 6?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 5

Technique: Rule 2 (Two Faces Common).

  • The common faces in both views are 2 and 3.
  • In the first view, the remaining third face is 6.
  • In the second view, the remaining third face is 5.
  • Therefore, 6 must be opposite to 5.

Question 13

Correct Answer: Square (■)

Technique: Combination of Rules.

  • Compare dice 1 and 3. The common faces are Triangle (▲) and Circle (●).
  • By Rule 2, the remaining faces must be opposite. So, Star (★) is opposite Diamond (◆).
  • Now compare dice 1 and 2. The common face is Star (★).
  • By Rule 1, the adjacent faces are opposites. Clockwise from ★ in die 1: ▲ then ●. Clockwise from ★ in die 2: ■ then ◆.
  • This means ▲ is opposite ■, and ● is opposite ◆. Wait, this contradicts our first finding.
  • Let's re-try Rule 1. From die 1, neighbors of ★ are ▲ and ●. From die 2, neighbors are ■ and ◆. This gives us the pairs.
  • From die 3, we see ▲ is next to ● and ◆. We already know from die 1 that ▲ is next to ● and ★.
  • So, the neighbors of ▲ are: ●, ★, ◆. The only symbol left is ■. Therefore, ▲ must be opposite ■.

Section 3: Hidden Faces & Logic (5 Questions)

Question 21

A standard die is rolled twice. The sum of the numbers on top is 9. What could be the sum of the numbers on the bottom faces?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 5

Technique: Standard Die Logic.

  • Opposite faces on a standard die sum to 7.
  • The only ways to get a sum of 9 from two rolls are (3+6), (4+5).
  • Case 1: Top faces are 3 and 6. The opposite bottom faces would be 4 and 1. Their sum is 4 + 1 = 5.
  • Case 2: Top faces are 4 and 5. The opposite bottom faces would be 3 and 2. Their sum is 3 + 2 = 5.
  • In both possible cases, the sum of the bottom faces is 5.

Question 23

From the given net, what is the sum of the numbers on the faces adjacent to the face with the number 1?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 14

Technique: Logic with Nets.

  • First, find the face opposite to 1. Using the "Alternate" Rule, 1 is opposite 3.
  • A die has 6 faces. Every face is adjacent to 4 faces and opposite to 1 face.
  • The faces adjacent to 1 are all faces EXCEPT its opposite (3).
  • The adjacent faces are 2, 4, 5, and 6.
  • Their sum is 2 + 4 + 5 + 6 = 17.

Question 24

A cube is painted red on all faces. It is then cut into 27 smaller, identical cubes. How many of the smaller cubes have exactly one face painted red?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 6

Technique: Cube Logic.

  • 27 smaller cubes means the large cube was cut into a 3x3x3 arrangement.
  • Cubes with one face painted are the ones in the center of each face of the large cube.
  • A cube has 6 faces.
  • Therefore, there are 6 cubes with exactly one face painted red.

Question 25

Which number lies at the bottom face of die (3)?

Show Answer & Explanation

Correct Answer: 6

Technique: Multi-view analysis.

  • This is a tricky one. Dice 1 and 2 have no common faces. This means the faces of die 1 (1,2,3) must be opposite the faces of die 2 (4,5,6).
  • Now look at die 3, which shows (1, 3, 5).
  • From die 1, we see 1 is adjacent to 3. From die 3, we also see 1 is adjacent to 3.
  • Let's find the pairs. In die 1, going clockwise from the 1-3 edge, we see 2. In die 3, going clockwise from the 1-3 edge, we see 5. This implies 2 is opposite 5.
  • Now we have one pair (2/5). Looking at die 3 again, we see 1 and 3 are adjacent to 5. So their opposites must also be adjacent to 2.
  • Since 2 is opposite 5, the remaining pairs must be (1/6) and (3/4) OR (1/4) and (3/6).
  • Looking at die 3, 1 is adjacent to 5. If 1 is opposite 6, then 6 cannot be adjacent to 5. Die 2 shows 6 is adjacent to 5. This is consistent.
  • So, we have pairs: (2,5). From die 1, 1 is adjacent to 2,3. From die 3, 1 is adjacent to 3,5. Since 2 is opposite 5, this works. The adjacent faces to 1 are 2,3,5... and the opposite of 3. From die 2, 4 is adjacent to 5,6. The best way is to deduce all pairs. 2 is opp 5. From die 1, we can see 1,2,3. From die 3, 1,3,5. This confirms 1 and 3 are adjacent to both 2 and 5. This makes them form a band. 1-3-6-4-1. So 1 is opp 6, 3 is opp 4.
  • Pairs are: (1,6), (2,5), (3,4).
  • The top face of die (3) is 1. Its opposite is 6. Therefore, 6 is on the bottom face.
