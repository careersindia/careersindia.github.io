PARA JUMBLES (SENTENCE REARRANGEMENT)

  • Solving Strategies
  • 50 Solved Examples

Strategies for Solving Para Jumbles

Concept: Para Jumbles are a set of related sentences that have been mixed up. The goal is to rearrange them into their original, logical order to form a coherent paragraph.

Key Strategies

  • 1. Find the Opening Sentence: This sentence introduces the main topic, person, or concept. It's usually a general statement and will not start with pronouns like 'he', 'it', 'they', or transition words like 'but', 'so', 'therefore', 'also'.
  • 2. Identify Mandatory Pairs: Look for sentences that have an unbreakable logical link.
    • Pronoun-Antecedent Link: A sentence with a pronoun (e.g., `he`, `its`, `they`) must follow the sentence that introduces the noun it refers to. (e.g., A sentence about `Ashoka` must come before a sentence starting with `He...`).
    • Cause and Effect Link: The sentence describing a cause will come before the sentence describing its effect.
    • Chronological Order: Arrange events based on the sequence of time (e.g., `first`, `then`, `finally`).
    • Idea-Example Link: A sentence presenting a general idea will come before the sentence that gives a specific example of it.
  • 3. Find the Concluding Sentence: This sentence summarizes the paragraph, provides a final thought, or states a conclusion. It often starts with words like `Thus`, `Therefore`, `Hence`, or `Finally`.
  • 4. Use the Options (if available): In multiple-choice questions, you can test the given sequences to see which one creates the most logical and coherent paragraph.

50 Solved Examples

  1. A. He was a great leader who was loved by his people.
    B. This kingdom was located in the fertile plains of the north.
    C. Ashoka was one of the greatest emperors of India.
    D. There was once a vast kingdom known as Magadha.
    Correct Sequence: DBCA
    Explanation: D is the best opening sentence as it introduces the broad topic 'Magadha'. B follows D as 'This kingdom' refers to Magadha (DB pair). C introduces the specific character 'Ashoka'. A must follow C as the pronoun 'He' refers to Ashoka (CA pair).
  2. A. As a result, they are better able to handle stress and anxiety.
    B. This is because it helps them to develop a sense of discipline and focus.
    C. Regular physical activity is essential for the mental well-being of students.
    D. It also releases endorphins, which have mood-boosting effects.
    Correct Sequence: CDBA
    Explanation: C is the opening sentence, introducing the main idea. D follows C, as 'It also' adds another benefit of physical activity. B follows D, explaining the reason behind the benefits ('This is because...'). A provides the final conclusion or result ('As a result...').
  3. A. However, this is not a sustainable solution in the long run.
    B. Many cities around the world are facing severe water shortages.
    C. One immediate response has been to extract more groundwater.
    D. This is due to a combination of climate change, population growth, and inefficient water management.
    Correct Sequence: BDCA
    Explanation: B introduces the problem. D explains the cause of the problem mentioned in B ('This is due to...'). C provides a response to the problem. A critiques the response in C ('However, this is not sustainable...').
  4. A. Their bright colors and intricate patterns are a treat for the eyes.
    B. They are found in diverse habitats, from tropical rainforests to temperate meadows.
    C. Butterflies are some of the most beautiful insects in the world.
    D. This beauty, however, is short-lived as most have a lifespan of only a few weeks.
    Correct Sequence: CBAD
    Explanation: C is the clear opening sentence, introducing butterflies. B provides more information about them. A follows, elaborating on their beauty mentioned in C. D presents a contrast ('however') to the beauty mentioned in A.
  5. A. This process is known as photosynthesis.
    B. They use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to create their own food.
    C. Green plants are unique among living organisms.
    D. The food they produce, glucose, provides energy for their growth.
    Correct Sequence: CBDA
    Explanation: C introduces the topic of green plants. B explains what makes them unique. D explains the result of the process mentioned in B. A gives the name to the process described in B and D.
  6. A. Consequently, the team's morale was very low.
    B. They had not won a single match in the entire tournament.
    C. The coach decided to implement a new training strategy.
    D. He believed this change would boost their confidence.
    Correct Sequence: BACD
    Explanation: B states the problem. A gives the result of the problem in B. C presents the solution. D explains the reason behind the solution in C ('this change').
  7. A. It is a time for feasting, fasting, and spiritual reflection.
    B. One of the most important festivals in Islam is Eid.
    C. Finally, it culminates in a day of celebration and charity.
    D. It marks the end of Ramadan, the holy month of fasting.
    Correct Sequence: BDAC
    Explanation: B is the introductory sentence. D provides more specific information about Eid mentioned in B ('It marks...'). A describes the activities during this time. C provides the concluding statement ('Finally...').
  8. A. The invention of the printing press was a pivotal moment in human history.
    B. Before this invention, books were handwritten and extremely rare.
    C. This allowed for the mass production of books, making knowledge accessible to all.
    D. It was invented by Johannes Gutenberg in the 15th century.
    Correct Sequence: ADBC
    Explanation: A introduces the main topic. D provides specific details (who and when) about the invention in A. B describes the situation *before* the invention. C explains the *result* of the invention.
  9. A. These tiny particles can penetrate deep into the lungs and cause serious health issues.
    B. Air pollution is a major environmental concern in modern cities.
    C. One of the most dangerous components of this pollution is particulate matter (PM2.5).
    D. Therefore, governments are implementing strict regulations to control emissions.
    Correct Sequence: BCAD
    Explanation: B is the general opening statement. C specifies a component of the pollution mentioned in B. A explains the danger of the component mentioned in C ('These tiny particles'). D is the concluding sentence, stating the solution ('Therefore...').
  10. A. Ultimately, this approach leads to a more engaged and motivated workforce.
    B. Instead of using a top-down approach, many modern companies are adopting a collaborative management style.
    C. They encourage employees at all levels to contribute ideas and take ownership of their projects.
    D. This involves open communication and mutual respect between managers and their teams.
    Correct Sequence: BCDA
    Explanation: B introduces the new management style. C explains what 'they' (the companies in B) do. D elaborates on this style ('This involves...'). A provides the final outcome ('Ultimately...').
  11. A. This nectar is a sugary fluid that provides them with energy.
    B. Bees play a crucial role in pollination.
    C. As they travel from flower to flower, they collect nectar.
    D. While collecting it, pollen sticks to their bodies and is transferred to other flowers.
    Correct Sequence: BCAD
    Explanation: B introduces the role of bees. C describes their action. A defines 'nectar' from C. D explains how pollination happens 'While collecting it' (it refers to nectar).
  12. A. In contrast, a pessimist tends to see the negative side of things.

    D. For example, they might view a failed project as a valuable learning experience.
    Correct Sequence: BDCA
    Explanation: B defines an optimist. D gives an example of the optimist's view. C explains the benefit of the mindset in B and D ('This mindset'). A provides a contrast to the main topic.
  13. A. The first step is to identify your long-term goals.

    D. Finally, prioritize these tasks and create a daily schedule.

  14. A. It was a dark and stormy night.

  15. A. He then presented his findings to the scientific community.

    Correct Sequence: BDCA
    Explanation: This follows a clear chronological order. B is the first action. D ('Next...') follows B ('it' refers to the hypothesis). C ('After months...') is the result of the experiments. A ('then...') is the final step.
