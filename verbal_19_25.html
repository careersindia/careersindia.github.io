Theory: Coded Relations

TYPE 3: CODED RELATIONS (EQUATION BASED)

In this type of questions, relations are represented using particular symbols and codes. The information provided has to be decoded for making family flowchart. This flow chart then helps us to answer the questions based on relationships between different persons.

Question: Example 1

Study the following information carefully to answer the given questions. If:

  • A $ B means A is the father of B
  • A @ B means B is the mother of A
  • A * B means A is the wife of B
  • A & B means A is the sister of B
  • A # B means B is the daughter of A

A) In A*B$C#D@E&F, how is 'B' related to 'D'?

  1. Son
  2. Daughter
  3. Father
  4. Grandfather
  5. Cannot be determined
Explanation: Question A

Let's decode the relations:

  • A * B → A is the wife of B. So, B is the husband (male).
  • B $ C → B is the father of C.
  • C # D → D is the daughter of C. So, C is D's parent.
From this, B is the father of C, and C is the parent of D. This makes B the grandfather of D.

B) In A*B$C#D@E&F, how is 'C' related to 'E'?

  1. Mother
  2. Husband
  3. Sister
  4. Daughter
  5. Uncle
Explanation: Question B

Let's continue decoding from the same expression:

  • C # D → D is the daughter of C.
  • D @ E → E is the mother of D.
If D is the daughter of C, and E is the mother of D, then C must be the father of D. This makes C and E a married couple, where C is the husband and E is the wife.

Answer: Question B

From the family tree it is obvious that 'C' and 'E' are a married couple where 'C' is the male partner while 'E' is the female partner. Hence, the correct answer is option (b) Husband.

Theory: Example 2 Codes

If:

  • P + Q means P is the husband of Q
  • P @ Q means P is the sister of Q
  • P x Q means P is the mother of Q
  • P $ Q means P is the father of Q
Question: Example 2

Which of the following shows D is the son of A?

Page 16 of 309
Options for Example 2
  1. A + B x C @ D $ E
  2. D x B + C @ A + E
  3. B + C x A $ D @ E
  4. C x B + A @ D $ E
Explanation: Solution to Example 2

We need to find an expression where D is the son of A. This means A must be a parent of D, and D must be male. Let's analyze option (a):

  • A + B → A is the husband of B. (A is male, B is female)
  • B x C → B is the mother of C.
  • C @ D → C is the sister of D. (C and D are siblings)
  • D $ E → D is the father of E. (D is male)

From the decoding: B is the mother of C and D. A is the husband of B, so A is the father of C and D. We also know D is male. Therefore, D is the son of A. Option (a) is correct.

PRACTICE QUESTIONS

Practice Question 1

If 'A + B' means 'A is father of B', 'A – B' means 'A is mother of B', 'A * B' means 'A is brother of B' and 'A % B' means 'A is sister of B', then how is Q related to S in 'P + Q * R – S'?

  1. Husband
  2. Uncle
  3. Brother
  4. Father
Answer

Correct Answer: b) Uncle

Explanation
  • P + Q → P is the father of Q.
  • Q * R → Q is the brother of R.
  • R – S → R is the mother of S.

Q is the brother of R, and R is the mother of S. This means Q is the maternal uncle of S.

Practice Question 2

If 'P x Q' means 'P is the father of Q'; 'P + Q' means 'P is the mother of Q'; 'P – Q' means 'P is the brother of Q'; 'P * Q' means 'P is the sister of Q'.

A) If A × B + C * D, then A is D's

  1. Father
  2. Grandmother
  3. Grandfather
  4. sister
Answer

Correct Answer: c) Grandfather

Explanation
  • A × B → A is the father of B.
  • B + C → B is the mother of C.
  • C * D → C is the sister of D.

A is the father of B. B is the mother of C and D. Therefore, A is the father of the mother of D, which makes A the maternal grandfather of D.

Using the same codes as before.

B) Which of the following shows that B is the aunt of E?

  1. A + B – C * D × E
  2. A × B * C + D – E
  3. A * B × C * D - E
  4. none of the above
Answer

Correct Answer: b) A × B * C + D – E

Explanation

Let's analyze option (b): A × B * C + D – E

  • A x B → A is father of B.
  • B * C → B is sister of C. (B is female).
  • C + D → C is mother of D.
  • D – E → D is brother of E.

B is the sister of C, and C is the mother of E. The sister of one's mother is their aunt. So, B is the aunt of E.

Practice Question 3

Each of these questions is based on the following information:

  1. X @ Y means X is the father of Y
  2. X & Y means X is the sister of Y.
  3. X # Y means X is the son of Y.

A) Which of the following shows the relation that F is the husband of C?

  1. C # B @ F @ E
  2. B @ F @ E # C
  3. C & B # F # E
  4. E # B @ F @ C
Answer

Correct Answer: b) B @ F @ E # C

Explanation

Let's analyze option (b): B @ F @ E # C

  • B @ F → B is father of F.
  • F @ E → F is father of E. (F is male).
  • E # C → E is son of C.

F is the father of E, and E is the son of C. This means F and C are the parents of E. Since F is the father (male), C must be the mother (female). Therefore, F is the husband of C.

Using the same codes as before.

B) Which of the following shows the relation that Q is the grandfather of R?

  1. S & P @ Q # R
  2. Q & R # P # S
  3. Q @ S & P @ R
  4. None of these
Answer

Correct Answer: c) Q @ S & P @ R

Explanation

Let's analyze option (c): Q @ S & P @ R

  • Q @ S → Q is father of S. (Q is male).
  • S & P → S is sister of P.
  • P @ R → P is father of R.

Q is the father of P (since S and P are siblings and Q is S's father). P is the father of R. Therefore, Q is the father of R's father, which makes Q the grandfather of R.
