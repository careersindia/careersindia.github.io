Problems on Trains

Core Concept 1: Unit Conversion

Question

What are the essential conversion factors for speed in train problems?

Answer

The two key conversion factors are for changing km/hr to m/s and vice-versa.

Explanation

  • To convert speed from km/hr to m/s, multiply by 5/18.
  • To convert speed from m/s to km/hr, multiply by 18/5.

This is derived from: 1 km = 1000 m and 1 hr = 3600 s. The ratio is 1000/3600 = 10/36 = 5/18.

Important Notes: The speaker emphasizes, "This is the first and most important step. In almost every train problem, the distance will be in meters (length of the train) and the speed will be in km/hr. You MUST make the units consistent. Memorize 5/18 and 18/5. This will save you from doing the full conversion every time."

Core Concept 2: Relative Speed

Question

How do you calculate the relative speed of two objects (like trains)?

Answer

It depends on their direction of travel.

Explanation

  • Same Direction: If two trains are moving in the same direction with speeds S1 and S2, their relative speed is the difference of their speeds: |S1 - S2|.
  • Opposite Directions: If two trains are moving towards each other or away from each other (opposite directions), their relative speed is the sum of their speeds: S1 + S2.

Important Notes: "Think about it logically," the speaker says. "If you are on a train and another train overtakes you in the same direction, it seems to pass slowly. That's the difference in speed. But if a train passes you from the opposite direction, it goes by very fast. That's the sum of the speeds. Same direction means subtract, opposite direction means add."

Question 1: Train Crossing a Stationary Object of No Length

Question

A 100-meter long train is running at a speed of 30 km/hr. Find the time it takes to pass a man standing near the railway line.

Answer

12 seconds.

Explanation

When a train crosses an object with negligible length (like a man, pole, or tree), the distance the train must cover is equal to its own length.

  1. Distance (D): Length of the train = 100 m.
  2. Speed (S): 30 km/hr. We must convert this to m/s.
    • Speed in m/s = 30 * (5/18) = 150/18 = 25/3 m/s.
  3. Time (T): Using the formula T = D / S.
    • T = 100 / (25/3) = 100 * (3/25) = 4 * 3 = 12 seconds.

Question 2: Train Crossing a Stationary Object of Length

Question

A train is moving at a speed of 132 km/hr. If the length of the train is 110 meters, how long will it take to cross a railway platform 165 meters long?

Answer

7.5 seconds.

Explanation

When a train crosses an object with significant length (like a platform, bridge, or another train), the total distance to be covered is the sum of the length of the train and the length of the object.

  1. Total Distance (D): Length of Train + Length of Platform = 110 m + 165 m = 275 m.
  2. Speed (S): 132 km/hr. Convert to m/s.
    • Speed in m/s = 132 * (5/18) = 22 * (5/3) = 110/3 m/s.
  3. Time (T): T = D / S.
    • T = 275 / (110/3) = 275 * (3/110) = 2.5 * 3 = 7.5 seconds.

Question 3: Two Trains in Opposite Directions

Question

Two trains, 137 meters and 163 meters in length, are running towards each other on parallel lines, one at the rate of 42 km/hr and another at 48 km/hr. In what time will they clear each other from the moment they meet?

Answer

12 seconds.

Explanation

  1. Total Distance (D): The total distance is the sum of the lengths of both trains.
    • D = 137 m + 163 m = 300 m.
  2. Relative Speed (S): Since they are moving in opposite directions, we add their speeds.
    • Relative Speed = 42 km/hr + 48 km/hr = 90 km/hr.
    • Convert to m/s: 90 * (5/18) = 5 * 5 = 25 m/s.
  3. Time (T): T = D / S.
    • T = 300 / 25 = 12 seconds.

Important Notes: The speaker reinforces the core concepts: "See how the rules combine? To find the distance, you add the lengths because it's two trains. To find the speed, you add the speeds because they are moving in opposite directions. Then it's just a simple Time = Distance / Speed calculation."
