ANALOGY

Theory: Introduction

An analogy is something that shows how two things are alike.

In questions related to Analogy, options are given, which may or may not be associated with each other and candidates need to find either the odd option or the similar option from the given set.

TIPS AND TRICKS TO SOLVE REASONING ANALOGY

  • Good observation, judgement and ability to relate one thing with another are pre-requisites for success in analogy problems.
  • Judge the pattern being followed in the given analogy.
  • For numerical questions, squares, cubes, multiplication, addition, etc. based patterns may be followed. So, ensure that you recheck these patterns well.
  • Having knowledge of basic GK and English Vocabulary is very important for these questions.

TYPES OF ANALOGY

  • Simple Word Analogy
  • Choosing the Analogous Pair
  • Multiple Word Analogy
  • Number Analogy
  • Alphabet Analogy
  • Mixed Analogy

TYPE 1: SIMPLE WORD ANALOGY

This type of analogy can involve questions based on any type of relationship you can think of. Some of the examples could be:

  • Country and Capital
  • State and Capital
  • Country and Currency
  • Quantity and Unit
  • Animal and Young one
  • Male and Female
  • Animals and their Sounds
  • Animals and their Shelters
  • Workers and their Tools
  • Workers and their Working Place
  • Word and Synonym
  • Word and Antonym
  • Cause and Effect
  • Study and Topic etc.
Question: Example 1

Eyes : Ophthalmologist :: Heart : ?

  1. Endocrinologist
  2. Nephrologist
  3. Cardiologist
  4. Neurologist

Solution: (c) Cardiologist

Page 148 of 309
Explanation: Solution to Example 1

As Eye related disorders are checked by an Ophthalmologist, similarly, Heart related disorders are checked by a Cardiologist.

Question: Example 2

Pulp : Paper :: Hemp : ? (SSC 2001)

Solution: (c) Rope

As Pulp is used to make Paper, similarly, Hemp is used to make a rope.

Question: Example 3

Chennai : Tamil Nadu :: Gandhinagar : ?

Solution: (b) Gujarat

Chennai is the capital of Tamil Nadu. Similarly, Gandhinagar is the capital of Gujarat.

Theory: Mixed Analogy

In this type of analogy, there is a relation between a given group of alphabets and certain numbers. This relation can be based on either the position of alphabets and letters or the sum or product of their positions.

Question: Example 1

F : 216 :: L : ? (SSC-CGL 2013)

  1. 1728
  2. 1700
  3. 1600
  4. 1723

The position of F is 6th in the alphabet. Also 6³ = 216. Similarly, the position of L is 12th in the alphabet.

Hence, 12³ = 1728. So, the correct answer is (a) 1728.

T(20) / J(10) = 2. Similarly, X(24) / H(8) = 3.
